# TinyML-based-Hybrid-underwater-communication-system-using-Acoustic-and-Optical-Li-Fi-Communication-
The proposed project presents a TinyML-based hybrid underwater communication system designed for Internet of Underwater Things (IoUT) applications. Traditional underwater communication systems face several challenges such as high communication delay, limited bandwidth, high energy consumption, and unreliable long-range data transmission in harsh underwater environments. To overcome these limitations, the proposed system integrates both acoustic and optical Li-Fi communication technologies with TinyML intelligence for adaptive and energy-efficient underwater networking.

In this architecture, underwater sensor nodes continuously monitor environmental parameters such as water quality, pollution levels, temperature, and seismic activity. The collected data are transmitted to an underwater sink node embedded with a TinyML model. The TinyML intelligence analyzes the incoming sensor data and dynamically selects the most suitable communication method based on environmental conditions and data priority. During emergency situations or turbid water conditions, acoustic communication is used to send reliable long-range alerts to the surface substation. For normal monitoring and large-volume data transfer, high-speed optical Li-Fi communication is established with Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUVs). The solar-powered AUVs collect and deliver the data efficiently, improving communication speed, reliability, energy efficiency, and overall underwater network performance.
